Distribute software
from your own APT repository

Your Personal Package Archive (“PPA”) simplifies publishing your software. Now you no longer need to ask testers on Ubuntu or Debian to compile your code from source. Instead, upload a source package to Launchpad and it will build then publish an installable binary in your own APT repository.

Installing your package is just as easy as with any other Ubuntu software and when you publish a new version your users receive automatic updates.

Automatic software distribution - with updates!

Publishing your software into a PPA is the best way to make sure it will reach a wide audience of Debian and Ubuntu users.

Instead of downloading individual packages, the archive enables your users to retrieve all your packages and their dependencies from a single reliable source.

When you publish a new version of one of your packages, Ubuntu notifies all of your users that an update is available and it can even automatically install the updated version for them if they prefer. This helps to keep all your users up to date, especially if you want to push the latest code to beta testers.

Team PPAs

Teams can have their own package archives.

Any member of a team can upload new packages to its PPA, making it a great way for groups of people to publish software collectively. The Kubuntu project did just that to distribute their first builds of the KDE 4 environment. If you have a team of developers working on a set of related packages, consider setting up a team PPA as a way to simplify coordination across each of those pieces of software.

Multiple architectures

Launchpad builds your PPA packages for multiple architectures automatically.

Even if you don’t have the hardware yourself, Launchpad will create binaries for x86, AMD64 and lpia architectures, with more coming in the future.
