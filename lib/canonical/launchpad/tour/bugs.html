Cross-project bug tracking.

When projects share free software code they share its bugs. Inevitably, the same bug is reported and discussed in different places.

Launchpad brings the discussion to you: on one page you can see how the bug affects different communities, whether they're tracking the bug in Launchpad, Bugzilla, Trac, Sourceforge, or elsewhere. You can even share a comment history across project-boundaries, bringing everyone together to find a fix.

Shared bug reports, independent statuses

By sharing bug reports and their comment histories across communities, Launchpad lets everyone who’s interested work together to find a fix.

Each group — whether projects or distributions — can track how the bug affects them by recording their own status, importance and assignee.

There’s even a “Bugs fixed elsewhere” report that shows which of your bugs are marked fixed in other communities, so you know where to look for low-hanging fruit.

As an example: someone discovers a Python bug while using Ubuntu’s Twisted package. While each community — Ubuntu, Twisted and Python — tracks the bug independently, Launchpad brings the bug statuses and conversations together into a single collaborative effort to fix the problem for everyone.

Launchpad works with these external bug trackers: Bugzilla, Trac, Sourceforge, Roundup, Mantis, RT and Debian BTS. More to come.

Delegate triage and planning

Delegate day to day bug management to trusted members of your project community.

The Bug Supervisor role is a convenient way to delegate bug management to a team or individual. They can set each bug’s importance and target them to future releases.

You can manage permissions in the bug tracker to balance the efficiency of an open community with the control of careful delegation. Stay on top of security issues by assigning a dedicated Security Contact and keep security bugs private until you’ve released a fix.

Bugs by email

Report, edit, assign and target bugs by email.

If you prefer, you can manage bug reports entirely through your mail client, updating bugs by replying to email notifications.

Stay informed of the bugs that interest you with email and Atom feed subscriptions. Launchpad sends email updates to everyone who’s interested in a bug, such as the initial reporter or the person assigned to work on a fix. You can also subscribe to individual bugs or all the bugs associated with a particular project or distribution package. Bug mail goes both ways: file new bugs, make comments, update statuses, add attachments and more by email.

Found a fix? Publish a branch

You no longer need to swap dead patches in bug comments. Instead, link bug reports directly to Bazaar branches of code containing the latest version of your fix.

Other community members can download your branches and test them without the hassle of applying patches. Thanks to Bazaar, it’s easy to merge the branch containing your bug fix into your project’s trunk. Bazaar branches are like "living patches" - a single URL points to the latest version of the fix and a single command merges it into your trunk branch.
